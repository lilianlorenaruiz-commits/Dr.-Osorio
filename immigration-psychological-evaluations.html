Immigration Services

Immigration Psychological Evaluations

Structured documentation for asylum, hardship waivers, VAWA, and other immigration matters

Immigration psychological evaluations support immigration cases by providing a structured psychological evaluation report that documents the emotional and psychological impact of an individual's experience.

At Dr. Claudia Osorio Psychological Services, we conduct evaluations for asylum, hardship waivers, VAWA, and other immigration matters. Each evaluation follows a structured, evidence-based process to produce clear, clinically grounded findings that can support legal documentation and case preparation.

For Whom

Who This Evaluation Is For

Individuals and Families

This evaluation may be appropriate if you or a family member are involved in an immigration process and need psychological documentation to support your case.

Common situations include:

  • Asylum and Convention Against Torture (CAT)
  • VAWA and domestic violence evaluations
  • Hardship waiver evaluations
  • U-Visa and T-Visa cases
  • Citizenship Examination Waiver (N-648)

Evaluations are conducted via telehealth when applicable, depending on PSYPACT state participation, provider licensure, and case requirements.

Attorneys and Law Firms

This service supports attorneys and law firms that require structured psychological documentation for immigration cases.

We provide:

  • Immigration psychological evaluation reports aligned with case requirements
  • Clear clinical findings for legal documentation
  • Experience with asylum, N-648, hardship waivers, VAWA, U-Visa, T-Visa, and related visa cases
  • Coordination with legal timelines across PSYPACT jurisdictions when applicable

The Evaluation

What the Evaluation Includes

Each immigration psychological evaluation follows a structured clinical process designed to produce a clear, professionally documented psychological report for immigration-related legal documentation.

Clinical Interview Personal history and immigration context, gathered in a structured conversation.
Psychological Assessment Emotional, cognitive, and behavioral functioning, evaluated with established methods.
Trauma-Informed When applicable, the evaluation is conducted with trauma-informed clinical practice.
Records Review Supporting records and relevant documentation are reviewed as part of the evaluation.

Final Deliverable: a comprehensive psychological evaluation report for immigration cases

  • Clear clinical findings and professional interpretation
  • Documentation that may support USCIS petitions and legal proceedings
  • Structured and clinically grounded understanding of the individual's experience within the immigration case
