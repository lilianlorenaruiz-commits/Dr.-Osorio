Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 17, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how Dr. Claudia Osorio Psychological Services (the "Practice", "we", "our") collects, uses, and protects information submitted through the website psychevaluations.net. This policy applies to the website only. Protected Health Information (PHI) collected during the clinical psychological evaluation process is governed by the Notice of Privacy Practices provided at the start of clinical engagement, not by this policy.

Information We Collect

When you contact us through this website we may collect the following information that you choose to provide: your name, email address, phone number, law firm or organization name (if applicable), the U.S. state or jurisdiction relevant to your case, the type of evaluation requested, and a brief case description. We do not request or knowingly collect Social Security numbers, financial information, or clinical Protected Health Information through this website.

How We Use Information

We use the information you provide to respond to your inquiry, schedule an initial free call, determine whether the requested evaluation fits within our scope of practice and licensure, and coordinate next steps. We do not use this information for marketing, profiling, or any purpose unrelated to responding to your inquiry.

Form Submissions and Email

The provider referral form on the For Attorneys page opens your default email application with a pre-filled draft addressed to admin@drosoriopsych.com. The form does not transmit data through this website. The email is sent through your own email service, not through us. Please do not include client identifiers, case numbers, or other sensitive details in the form body. This website does not provide a HIPAA-secure communication channel.

Information Sharing

We do not sell, rent, or trade the information collected through this website. We share information only with the licensed clinicians and administrative staff at the Practice who need it to respond to your inquiry. We may disclose information when required by law, court order, or other legal process.

Data Retention

Inquiries received through this website are retained only as long as needed to respond to the inquiry, follow up on next steps, and meet professional record-keeping obligations applicable to a licensed psychology practice.

Cookies and Analytics

This website does not use tracking cookies. We do not run third-party analytics, advertising pixels, or behavioral tracking scripts. Web fonts are loaded from Google Fonts, which may receive technical request information (such as your IP address) for the purpose of serving the font files.

Children

This website is intended for adults. We do not knowingly collect information from individuals under 18 through this website. Clinical evaluations of minors are arranged through a parent or legal guardian during the intake process.

External Links

This website links to external resources, including the Psychology Interjurisdictional Compact (PSYPACT) website. We are not responsible for the privacy practices of external websites. Please review the privacy policies of those sites before submitting information to them.

Security

We take reasonable steps to protect the information submitted through this website. However, no method of internet transmission or electronic storage is fully secure. You assume the inherent risk of internet communication when you contact us through this website.

Your Rights

You may request that we delete the information you submitted through this website by contacting us at admin@drosoriopsych.com. State law (Massachusetts, Connecticut, Florida) and federal law may grant additional rights regarding your information; we will respond consistent with applicable law.

Updates to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The date at the top of this page reflects the most recent revision. Material changes will be flagged at the top of the page for at least 30 days after publication.
