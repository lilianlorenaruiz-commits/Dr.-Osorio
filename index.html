Psychological Evaluation Services

Immigration Psychological Evaluations

Our immigration psychological evaluations support individuals undergoing immigration proceedings by providing psychological reports that document the emotional impact of their immigration circumstances and help strengthen their cases.

These evaluations may include asylum and CAT evaluations, VAWA and domestic violence evaluations, T-Visa and U-Visa evaluations, hardship waiver evaluations, N-648 evaluations, and selected immigration court-related forensic assessments.

Forensic Psychological Evaluations

Our forensic psychological evaluations are designed to address psycho-legal issues in criminal, civil, and family court cases.

These evaluations provide structured psychological assessment, objective clinical analysis, and professionally documented findings that may support legal understanding and decision-making.

For Attorneys / Legal

For attorneys and legal professionals, Dr. Claudia Osorio Psychological Services provides specialized immigration and forensic psychological evaluations for legal cases across the U.S.

Our evaluations support a wide range of legal and immigration matters, including:

  • Asylum and hardship waiver evaluations
  • VAWA, U-Visa, and T-Visa cases
  • N-648 medical certification for disability exceptions
  • Competency and mitigation evaluations in immigration court
  • Risk assessments, psychosexual evaluations, and other forensic-related cases

Our team delivers objective, culturally informed, and trauma-aware evaluations with clear, professionally structured reports that support legal strategy and case documentation.

Through PSYPACT-authorized telepsychology services, we expand access across all 43 PSYPACT participating states — no matter where your client is located. Services are available in English and Spanish, with additional language support when needed.

How It Works

How the Process Works

1

Schedule Free Call

The process begins with a free call to understand the request, answer initial questions, and identify the most appropriate type of psychological evaluation.

This step helps clarify whether the evaluation is related to immigration, psychological concerns, or a forensic-related matter, and allows us to review timeline, jurisdiction, and next steps.

2

Evaluation

The psychological evaluation process is structured, collaborative, and evidence-based. Depending on the purpose of the assessment, it may include:

  • Clinical interviews
  • Standardized psychological testing
  • Review of relevant records or documentation
  • Assessment of emotional, cognitive, behavioral, and psychological functioning

Each evaluation is designed to develop a clear and accurate understanding of the case or situation.

3

Report and Next Steps

Following the evaluation, clients receive a comprehensive psychological evaluation report with clearly documented findings and, when appropriate, professional recommendations.

This report may support:

  • Diagnostic clarification
  • Immigration-related documentation
  • Legal or court-related matters
  • Next steps for care, support, or decision-making
